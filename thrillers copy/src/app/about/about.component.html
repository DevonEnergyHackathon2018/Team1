Background

EHS activities are marked by a number of key metrics which are categorized primarily as either "leading" or "lagging." Leading metrics are data points taken before any given event occurs and by nature are forward-facing. Lagging metrics, on the other hand, are data points taken after any given event occurs.

In this tool, we analyze leading metrics and EHS events to identify the correlation between EHS activities and their ability to prevent negative EHS events.

Methodology

We evaluated two data sets: Exposure Reduction Activity and Event Summary. After moving these datasets into Databricks, we regressed each variable to identify where correlations exist between pre-event safety activities and negative events recorded. Any correlation above(+/-).5 was considered significant.

Using the correlations, we assigned points to each of the pre-event activities based on their impact on safety.
